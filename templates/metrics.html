Weak verbs

These are most common English verbs with vague meaning. They don't trigger vivid imagery in readers' heads and usually require additional words to deliver a meaningful message. Light verbs are examples of weak verbs. Substituting weak verbs with more specific ones will liven most texts.

Examples: to be, to have, to do, to say, to go, to give.
